Journey Through the Milestones of a Revolutionary Legacy

1867 - Born Maria Sklodowska in Warsaw, Poland.

1891 - Moves to Paris to attend the University of Paris (Sorbonne), where she studies physics and mathematics.

1893 - Earns her degree in physics, and begins work in an industrial laboratory of Professor Gabriel Lippmann.

1894 - Earns her degree in mathematics and meets Pierre Curie. They begin collaborating on research.

1895 - Marries Pierre Curie.

1896 - Inspired by Henri Becquerel's discovery of radioactivity, Marie begins her own research into uranium rays.

1898 - Announces the discovery of two new elements, polonium and radium.

1903 - Receives the Nobel Prize in Physics alongside Pierre Curie and Henri Becquerel for their combined work on radioactivity. She becomes the first woman to win a Nobel Prize.

1906 - Pierre Curie dies in a tragic accident. Marie Curie continues their research and takes over his teaching position at the Sorbonne, becoming the first woman to teach at the institution.

1911 - Receives the Nobel Prize in Chemistry for her discovery of radium and polonium, making her the first person to win Nobel Prizes in two different scientific fields.

1914 - World War I begins. Marie Curie develops mobile radiography units, known as "Little Curies," to assist in battlefield medical care.

1918 - After the war, she continues her research and promotes the use of radium to alleviate suffering.

1921 - Travels to the United States to receive a gram of radium, gifted by American women to support her research.

1934 - Dies of aplastic anemia, likely caused by prolonged exposure to radiation during her research.
